## Social Selling Index (SSI)

### What It Is

The Social Selling Index (SSI) is LinkedIn's proprietary 0–100 score that measures how effectively a user builds their professional brand and leverages LinkedIn for social selling. It is updated daily and broken into **four equal pillars of 25 points each:**

| Pillar | What It Measures |
|--------|----------------|
| **Establish your professional brand** | Profile completeness, thought leadership content, and how others perceive your expertise |
| **Find the right people** | Using LinkedIn search and recommendations to identify and connect with the right prospects or collaborators |
| **Engage with insights** | Sharing relevant content, commenting, and being an active participant in conversations in your feed |
| **Build relationships** | Growing your network strategically with quality connections, not just quantity |

**How to check your SSI:**
→ Visit [linkedin.com/sales/ssi](https://www.linkedin.com/sales/ssi) while logged in. The score is free to view for all LinkedIn members.

> ⚠️ **Context for the user — NOT computed from a PDF export.** SSI is a live metric
> that can only be seen by the account holder at the URL above. An audit from a
> PDF export cannot calculate or estimate your SSI.

---

### SSI Benchmark Table

| Score Range | Tier | Percentile | Interpretation |
|-------------|------|------------|---------------|
| 80–100 | Elite | Top 10% | Highly optimised profile + active selling/networking behaviour |
| 70–79 | Strong | Top 25% | Profile is solid; active engagement; room to polish |
| 60–69 | Good | Top 40% | Above average; clear gaps in one or two pillars |
| 40–59 | Average | Middle 50% | Profile or engagement habits need meaningful work |
| <40 | Low | Bottom 50% | Significant gaps — profile likely incomplete and/or inactive |

**Target:** Aim for **70+** as a baseline for job seekers, consultants, and personal brand builders. Creators and sales professionals should target **80+**.

---

### SSI Improvement Levers (by pillar)

| Pillar | Fastest wins |
|--------|-------------|
| Establish brand | Complete all profile sections to "All-Star" status; publish at least one post per week |
| Find people | Use LinkedIn Search with filters weekly; check "People Also Viewed" and second-degree connections |
| Engage with insights | Comment on 3–5 posts per day; share articles with original commentary |
| Build relationships | Send personalised connection requests; respond to all InMails and messages within 24 hours |

## Profile Visibility Targets

> ⚠️ **Context for the user — NOT computed from a PDF export.** All metrics in
> this section require access to LinkedIn's "Dashboard" panel (visible only when
> logged into your own profile) or Creator analytics.

### Core Visibility Benchmarks

| Metric | Minimum baseline | Strong | Elite |
|--------|-----------------|--------|-------|
| Weekly profile views | 20+ | 50+ | 200+ |
| Weekly search appearances | 10–15 | 30–50 | 100+ |
| Total connections | 500+ | 1,000+ | 5,000+ |
| Recommendations received | 3+ | 5+ | 10+ |
| Skills endorsed (top skill) | 10+ endorsements | 25+ | 99+ |

**Notes on these targets:**
- "500+ connections" is LinkedIn's own threshold for the "500+" badge — profiles below this can appear less established in recruiter searches.
- Weekly search appearances below 10 suggest the profile is under-indexed for target keywords. This is a text-fixable issue surfaced by the audit.
- Profile views spike 2–4× after publishing a post; weekly views should be evaluated on a rolling 4-week average, not a single spike week.

### Engagement Rate Formula

For LinkedIn content creators and personal brand builders, engagement rate is the primary content KPI:

```
Engagement Rate = (Reactions + Comments + Shares) / Impressions × 100
```

| Rate | Performance |
|------|------------|
| <1% | Low — content is not resonating or audience is mismatched |
| 1–2% | Below average — adjust content format or topic mix |
| 2–3% | Average for LinkedIn |
| 3–5% | Strong — above LinkedIn average; content strategy is working |
| 5%+ | Elite — viral territory; likely algorithm-boosted |

> ⚠️ **Context for the user — NOT computed from a PDF export.** Impressions
> data requires access to LinkedIn Creator analytics (desktop only, available
> after enabling Creator Mode). Individual post performance can be seen by the
> account holder only.

**Target:** 3%+ sustained engagement rate across your last 10 posts is the benchmark for creator mode.

## Industry-Specific Benchmark Adjustments

> ⚠️ **Context for the user — NOT computed from a PDF export.** These are
> calibration notes for interpreting the user's self-reported or live metrics —
> not numbers to fabricate from a static export.

These notes adjust expectations by industry. What looks "weak" in one sector may be "above average" in another.

---

### 💻 Tech / SaaS

| Metric | Industry context |
|--------|----------------|
| SSI target | 75+ is table stakes for anyone in sales, marketing, or leadership roles |
| Profile views | Competitive; 50+ weekly views is achievable with regular posts in product/engineering communities |
| Recommendations | 5+ is expected for senior roles; client-facing roles (sales, CS, consulting) should have client recommendations |
| Activity | LinkedIn is a primary distribution channel for thought leadership; posting 2–3×/week is the norm among top performers |
| Headline keywords | "SaaS," "B2B," specific tools (Salesforce, Figma, Kubernetes), specific roles (Staff Engineer, GTM, PLG) have high search volume |
| Top profile gap | Experience bullets with no ARR/revenue/growth metrics — quantification is expected and sets apart A-players |

---

### 🏦 Finance

| Metric | Industry context |
|--------|----------------|
| SSI target | 65–70 is solid; finance is more conservative about public social content |
| Profile views | Lower weekly baselines (15–30) are normal; finance hiring is heavily relationship-driven, not search-driven |
| Recommendations | Compliance constraints may limit endorsement specificity; generic recommendations are common and not a red flag here |
| Activity | Regulatory-sensitive industry — many professionals post sparingly; thought leadership still valued but expect lower volume |
| Headline keywords | CFA, CPA, CFA charterholder, "Investment Banking," "Private Equity," "Asset Management," "Risk," "FP&A" are high-signal search terms |
| Top profile gap | About section — finance professionals often leave it blank or use a one-liner; a well-written 200-word About is an immediate differentiator |

---

### 🏥 Healthcare

| Metric | Industry context |
|--------|----------------|
| SSI target | 55–65 is typical; healthcare professionals use LinkedIn less actively than tech counterparts |
| Profile views | 10–25 weekly is common; spikes typically come from conference attendance or published research |
| Recommendations | Peer and supervisor recommendations are high-value; patient-facing roles may have ethical constraints on client testimonials |
| Activity | Content is valued but must be HIPAA-aware and clinically conservative; LinkedIn articles work well for policy/public health perspectives |
| Headline keywords | Specific credentials (MD, RN, PA, DO), specialities (Oncology, Emergency Medicine), and roles (Chief Medical Officer, Clinical Researcher) dominate search |
| Top profile gap | Lack of education section detail — publications, residencies, fellowships, and board certifications should all be listed |

---

### 📣 Marketing

| Metric | Industry context |
|--------|----------------|
| SSI target | 75+ expected for anyone in demand gen, brand, or content; marketers are expected to "live on" LinkedIn |
| Profile views | 75–150+ weekly views is achievable; marketing professionals are among the most active users on the platform |
| Recommendations | Client and cross-functional recommendations are both valued; look for specific campaign results in recommendation text |
| Activity | Posting 3–5×/week is table stakes for content marketers and CMOs building thought leadership; engagement rate should be tracked weekly |
| Headline keywords | "Demand Generation," "B2B Marketing," "Content Strategy," "Growth Marketing," "SEO," "Paid Social," "CMO" — keyword-rich headlines are expected and rewarded |
| Top profile gap | Portfolio — case studies, campaign links, or media mentions rarely appear in profiles; adding them is an immediate differentiator |

---

### 🤝 Consulting

| Metric | Industry context |
|--------|----------------|
| SSI target | 75+ strongly recommended; consulting is a relationship business and SSI measures exactly this |
| Profile views | 30–80 weekly views is a reasonable target; spikes come from speaking engagements and published frameworks |
| Recommendations | Client recommendations are the gold standard here — every engagement should ideally result in one; aim for 5+ and rotate recency |
| Activity | Consultants who publish original frameworks, case studies (anonymised), and point-of-view posts gain inbound leads; 2–3×/week is a workable floor |
| Headline keywords | "Strategy," "Operations," "Fractional [Role]," "Executive Advisor," specific methodology names (Lean, OKRs, Agile), and industry verticals served |
| Top profile gap | About section fails to articulate the problem solved and the transformation delivered — consultants often write a bio, not a pitch |

---

### 🎨 Creative

| Metric | Industry context |
|--------|----------------|
| SSI target | 60–70 is solid; creatives often have stronger presence on Behance, Dribbble, or Instagram than LinkedIn |
| Profile views | Highly variable — spikes from viral posts or award recognition; steady 20–40 weekly is a healthy baseline |
| Recommendations | Client and art-director recommendations with specific project references are valued; "great to work with" adds less signal |
| Activity | Behind-the-scenes process content, finished work showcases, and opinion posts about craft perform strongly; LinkedIn's document format works well for portfolio slideshows |
| Headline keywords | Specific discipline terms (UX Designer, Brand Identity, Motion Graphics, Copywriter, Creative Director) + tools (Figma, After Effects, Premiere, Midjourney) + industry served |
| Top profile gap | Featured section — creative portfolios belong in the Featured section with project thumbnails, not buried in experience bullets |

## Benchmark Usage Rules for the Audit

1. **Never compute SSI, impressions, or engagement rate from a PDF export.** These are live metrics only. Reference the target numbers and direct the user to check them manually.

2. **Use benchmarks to frame severity.** A recommendation count of 2 is "weak" in consulting but "average" in finance — industry context changes the verdict.

3. **Surface the gap, not the raw number.** Don't just say "you have 3 recommendations." Say "you have 3 recommendations; in consulting, the target is 5+ client recommendations — adding 2 more would move you from below-average to strong."

4. **Link to where users can check live data:**
   - SSI: [linkedin.com/sales/ssi](https://www.linkedin.com/sales/ssi)
   - Profile views + search appearances: LinkedIn "Dashboard" panel on your own profile (desktop)
   - Post impressions + engagement: LinkedIn "Analytics" on each post (must be your own content)

5. **Flag "not assessable" as a to-do, not a zero.** When photo, banner, or activity can't be scored from the PDF, list them under a "Can't Score From Export — Check These Yourself" section with the target benchmarks inline.
